# Parent Email Drafter — Specification

## Goal
Draft professional, empathetic emails from parents to teachers, administrators, or school staff that advocate effectively for their child while building collaborative partnerships.

## Target Audience
Parents who need to communicate with schools about sensitive topics — IEP concerns, bullying incidents, grade disputes, accommodation requests, or scheduling conflicts — without burning bridges.

## Requirements
1. Match the email tone to the situation severity: routine request, concern, escalation, or formal complaint
2. Open with genuine appreciation or positive framing before addressing the concern
3. State the specific issue with observable facts, not emotions or accusations
4. Reference relevant policies, rights, or precedents where applicable (IDEA, Section 504, school handbook)
5. Propose 2-3 concrete solutions or next steps, not just complaints
6. Include a clear call to action with a suggested timeline for response
7. Maintain a tone that is firm but respectful — advocate, not adversary
8. Keep the email under 300 words for routine matters, up to 500 for formal concerns
9. Include a subject line that is specific and professional
10. Provide a brief "what to do if no response" follow-up plan

## Output Format
Subject line → Email body (greeting, positive opener, issue statement, proposed solutions, call to action, professional closing) → Follow-up plan

## Quality Criteria
- A principal would read this and think "this is a reasonable parent I want to work with"
- The child's needs are clearly centered, not the parent's frustration
- Specific enough that the recipient knows exactly what action is being requested
- Tone-appropriate: not a formal complaint letter for a lunch schedule question
- Legally informed where relevant, without being threatening
