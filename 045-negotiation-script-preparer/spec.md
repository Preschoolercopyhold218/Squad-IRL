# Negotiation Script Preparer — Specification

## Goal
Prepare a complete negotiation playbook with scripts, counterarguments, and psychological tactics for salary negotiations, price negotiations, or contract discussions — so the user walks in confident and walks out with a better deal.

## Target Audience
Professionals negotiating salary/raises, freelancers setting rates, consumers making major purchases (cars, homes, services), or anyone entering a negotiation feeling underprepared.

## Requirements
1. Research and establish the user's BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement) and walk-away point
2. Provide 3 opening scripts at different assertiveness levels: collaborative, confident, and aggressive
3. Anticipate the 5 most likely counterarguments and provide word-for-word responses to each
4. Include anchoring strategy with a specific first number and justification for it
5. Script silence techniques and pause points — when to stop talking and let them respond
6. Provide "if they say X, you say Y" decision tree for the most common negotiation branches
7. Include body language and delivery notes (tone, pacing, when to smile, when to be serious)
8. Prepare a graceful "walk-away" script and a "let me think about it" delay script
9. Research comparable data points (market rates, competitor offers, industry benchmarks) to support the ask
10. End with a closing script that locks in the agreement and confirms next steps

## Output Format
Pre-negotiation prep sheet → Opening scripts (3 levels) → Counterargument responses → Decision tree → Body language guide → Walk-away/delay scripts → Closing script

## Quality Criteria
- Scripts sound like natural human speech, not corporate templates
- Counterarguments address what the other side will ACTUALLY say, not strawmen
- Numbers are grounded in real market data, not aspirational
- Tactics are ethical — persuasive, not manipulative
- The user could rehearse these scripts out loud and feel confident
